My Photography Journey

My family is a mix of creativity and logical thinking. My father and much of his side of the family used to take photos and paint quite professionally, so I took inspiration from a drive to do something similarly to them. Photography has been a way for me to express my creative side and force me to use that side of my brain. As an Electrical Engineering major we dont really need to use our creativity unless were trying to imagine what an electromagnetic wave might look like and how it travels through space. Lately my girlfriend has pushed me to travel which i have been grateful for because it's allowing me to see the beauty of the world while i still have the energy.

Photographers Who Inspire Me

These photographers have influenced my style and approach to photography. Their work continues to inspire and teach me as I develop my own photographic voice. When I was starting out and trying to find where to start, I went to them for ideas.

Peter McKinnon

Peter McKinnon is a Canadian photographer and filmmaker who gained widespread popularity through his YouTube channel. His energetic approach to teaching photography and cinematography has inspired millions of creators worldwide. I particularly appreciate his emphasis on storytelling and finding beauty in everyday scenes.

What I've Learned from Peter

  • Dramatic Editing: Peter's high-contrast, moody editing style has influenced my approach to lightroom editing.
  • Creative B-roll: His techniques for capturing engaging supplementary footage has improved my video projects.
  • Gear Minimalism: Despite having access to premium equipment, Peter often emphasizes that creativity matters more than gear which was important to me starting off with just a camera and two lenses.
