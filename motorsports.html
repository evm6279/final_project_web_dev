My Motorsports Passion

From the roar of engines to the precision engineering, motorsports has always captivated my attention. Whether it's the technical aspects of vehicle performance or the thrill of speed, this passion has led me to own and ride motorcycles and drive performance cars. Join me in exploring my personal collection and my favorite aspects of motorsports culture.

My Vehicles

I'm fortunate to have two vehicles that satisfy different aspects of my passion for motorsports. Click the buttons below to learn more about each one!

My Motorcycle

My Yamaha MT-07 has been my faithful companion on countless rides. The raw torque and nimble handling make it perfect for both city commuting and weekend canyon carving. I've made a few modifications to enhance its performance and style, including a custom exhaust system and upgraded suspension.

Yamaha MT-07 Specifications

  • Engine: 689cc liquid-cooled DOHC 4-stroke inline twin-cylinder
  • Power: 74.8 hp @ 9,000 rpm
  • Torque: 50.2 lb-ft @ 6,500 rpm
  • Weight: 406 lbs (wet)
  • Suspension: 41mm telescopic fork (front), single shock (rear)
  • Brakes: Dual 282mm discs (front), 245mm disc (rear)
  • Fuel Capacity: 3.7 gallons
  • Seat Height: 31.7 inches

My modifications: Akrapovič exhaust, Öhlins rear shock, LED integrated tail light, and bar-end mirrors.

My Car

My car represents the perfect balance between daily usability and weekend performance. With enough power to keep things exciting but still comfortable for longer trips, it's been a joy to own. I've made several enhancements to improve handling and responsiveness while maintaining its reliability.

Car Specifications

  • Engine: 2.0L turbocharged inline-four
  • Power: 300 hp @ 6,500 rpm
  • Torque: 280 lb-ft @ 1,900 rpm
  • Transmission: 6-speed manual
  • Weight: 3,300 lbs
  • 0-60 mph: 5.4 seconds
  • Drivetrain: All-wheel drive

My modifications: Aftermarket exhaust, upgraded intercooler, Cold Air Intake, and Blow-off Valve.

MotoGP - The Pinnacle of Motorcycle Racing

MotoGP represents the absolute pinnacle of motorcycle racing technology and rider skill. These machines can exceed 220 mph and lean at angles that seem to defy physics. Here are some incredible moments from recent MotoGP seasons that showcase why this motorsport captivates millions of fans worldwide.

The extreme lean angles in MotoGP can exceed 60 degrees, with riders' knees and elbows dragging on the tarmac at speeds over 100 mph.

Race starts in MotoGP are intense moments where riders accelerate from 0 to 100 mph in just seconds, carefully managing wheelies and finding the perfect line.

Modern MotoGP bikes produce over 250 horsepower and weigh just 346 pounds, creating one of the highest power-to-weight ratios in motorsport.

Wheel-to-wheel battles in MotoGP require exceptional skill and bravery, with riders making split-second decisions at extreme speeds.

Formula 1 - The Pinnacle of Motorsport Engineering

Formula 1 represents the ultimate combination of engineering excellence, driver skill, and team strategy. These technological marvels accelerate from 0-60 mph in under 2.5 seconds and generate enough downforce to theoretically drive upside down. Here are some spectacular moments from recent F1 seasons that showcase why this motorsport is considered the most prestigious racing series in the world.

Modern F1 cars generate over 1,000 horsepower from compact 1.6-liter hybrid power units, demonstrating incredible engineering efficiency.

Race starts in F1 are crucial moments that can determine the outcome of an entire Grand Prix, with positions gained or lost in seconds.

During wet races, F1 drivers navigate at high speeds with severely limited visibility, relying on instinct and track knowledge.

Max Verstappen achieved 53 career wins, surpassing Ayrton Senna’s record, and set new benchmarks in Formula One, including the most wins in a single season and the longest unbroken sequence of victories​​.
